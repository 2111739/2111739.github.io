Essential Skills for Study and Employment

As an increasing number of people gain access to education through traditional and novel means, it is important to reflect on the effectiveness on different learning techniques. These techniques will feed into how employable students are by the end of their education and further on to their ability to develop their career. In order to start a career, future employers need to see candidates can demonstrate their knowledge, skills and experience. To retain knowledge, it is vital to revisit what you have learned so that it is accessible from long-term memory. For more details, see [1]. Tools such as OneNote allow learners to record information on a platform that is readily accessible, simplifying the revisiting and review stages of learning.

Time management is key to both study and employment. The planning process is the root of time management – structuring tasks and goals into an order of priorities allows high priority tasks to be singled out from distractions and low important tasks. [2] Soft skills are another key component in becoming employable, these are a set of core skills that employers seek out in every candidate. Problem solving, communication and problem solving skills contribute to a profile that will meet essential requirements from employers. [3]

The ability to manage time effectively appears again under leadership skills – in order to effectively manage a team, you must be able to appropriately delegate tasks to each member. A demonstration of the ability to research and understand research can be shown through conducting research and referencing sources that were used. [4]
